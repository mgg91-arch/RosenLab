Principal Investigator

Dr. Jennifer Rosen
MD, FACS

Professor of Surgery · Georgetown University School of Medicine

Jennifer Rosen, MD, FACS is Chief of Endocrine Surgery and Vice Chair for Research of the Department of Surgery at MedStar Washington Hospital Center. She serves on the Collaborative Endocrine Surgery Quality Improvement Program Committee of the American Association of Endocrine Surgeons (AAES) and sits as a representative of the College to the Commission on Cancer (COC), a program of the American College of Surgeons (ACS).

She has been active on numerous committees of the American Thyroid Association (ATA) and serves as Endocrine Section Editor for the Journal of Surgical Oncology. For more than five years, Dr. Rosen served as The Laszlo N. Tauber Assistant Professor of Surgery and Molecular Medicine at the Boston University School of Medicine.

Dr. Rosen specializes in the surgical treatment of diseases of the endocrine glands, including the thyroid, parathyroid and adrenal. She works closely with endocrinologists, nuclear medicine physicians and radiologists to determine proper testing and the best treatment options for each patient.

Her clinical interests include treating patients with complex, advanced or recurrent endocrine disease. Her research interests include bringing in new technology for diagnosing thyroid nodules, discriminating cancerous from benign nodules, improving genetic studies to predict thyroid cancer, and assembling large national databases to study survival in patients with thyroid cancer. Dr. Rosen has previously patented tools now in use at MedStar Washington Hospital Center.

Dr. Rosen's practice utilizes laparoscopic approaches to adrenal procedures and minimally invasive thyroid and parathyroid surgery techniques. She also uses nerve monitoring, intraoperative parathyroid hormone measurements, intraoperative ultrasound, and 4D-CT scan for localization of abnormal parathyroid glands.

Her translational research program bridges the operating room and the bench, investigating molecular drivers of thyroid carcinogenesis, optimizing intraoperative neuromonitoring, and developing predictive models for surgical risk stratification. She is a principal investigator on multiple NIH-funded trials and serves on the editorial boards of leading endocrine surgery journals.

"Every patient has their own story and their own history. We do our best to take care of each patient as a unique individual by tailoring their care to them. My goal is for my patients to feel very comfortable asking me questions."

Research Focus Areas

Our lab pursues fundamental and translational questions at the intersection of oncology, surgical technique, and endocrine physiology—with a unifying goal of improving patient outcomes.

01

Thyroid Cancer Molecular Mechanisms

Investigating BRAF, RAS, and RET oncogenic drivers in differentiated thyroid carcinoma to inform surgical decision-making and adjuvant therapy selection.

GenomicsPTCBRAF V600E
02

Intraoperative Neuromonitoring

Developing real-time continuous neural monitoring protocols to prevent recurrent laryngeal nerve injury during thyroid and parathyroid surgery.

RLNCIONMNerve Mapping
03

Minimally Invasive Approaches

Evaluating remote-access endoscopic and robotic thyroidectomy techniques for clinical equivalence, cosmesis, and patient-reported outcomes.

RoboticBABATOETVA
04

Primary Hyperparathyroidism

Outcomes research in parathyroidectomy for sporadic and MEN-associated disease, including focused versus bilateral exploration and long-term biochemical cure rates.

PHPTMEN1Parathyroid
05

Surgical Risk & Outcomes Modeling

Building machine-learning-assisted predictive models for post-thyroidectomy hypocalcemia, hematoma risk, and length of stay using national registry data.

MLNSQIPPrediction
06

Health Disparities in Thyroid Care

Examining racial, socioeconomic, and geographic disparities in access to guideline-concordant thyroid cancer surgery and surveillance across the DMV region.
